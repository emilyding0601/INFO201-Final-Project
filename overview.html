Overview

College Score Card Data

Our project focuses on propestive students giving them information on universities throughout the country. It contains a multitude of information about each university. This data can provides information on universities a prospective student may want or need in order to decide on what institution they might attend.

Data Set

The data set we decided to use was provided from https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/dat. Information for the universities is provided from 2006 to 2015. The map data is from 2015 only.

Here you will find lots of information about colleges across the United States. The data provided includes admission rates, SAT scores, cost of attendance, and diversity. The information we have provided is intended for people or parents of individuals who are looking to attend a university. The data spans from 2006 to 2015 which includes 7,409 observations from the different colleges around the US. The average number of colleges observed per year is approximately 685. There are search functions that allow you to interact with the visualizations to view any information desired.

We have decided to analyze some of the most important factors involved with a decision to aplly for admission to a college. Admission rates give an idea of how many students are accepted out of how many have applied. SAT scores provide the average SAT score for students admitted to the university. SAT scores are only one aspect of the admissions process. One of the most notable factors is the cost of attendance. Also, we wanted to explore the diversity among the universities.

Questions

  • What are the admission rates per university across the nation?
  • What are the average SAT scores for admitted persons?
  • What are the cost associated to the universities?
  • What is the diversity among the universities?
